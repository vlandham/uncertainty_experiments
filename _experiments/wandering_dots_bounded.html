Metaphors/Techniques

In this experiment, dots represent events that have occurred in a region where the points are restricted to movement within a polygon.

The higher the uncertainty, the larger the spatial region, and thus, the further the dots wander. The metaphor here is about events "spreading out" over their possible locations.

The above use a consistent number of dots to "fill" in an area. The number of dots could also be associated with a metric - such as event count or fatality count.
